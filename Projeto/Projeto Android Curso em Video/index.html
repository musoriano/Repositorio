Android's Maskot History

You probably now that the operational system Android, created by Google its one of the most popular mobile systems used in the hole world. But maybe you didn't now that their loved Maskot has a very interesting history behind it? Well, follow this article to learn more about this little robot.

The fist version

The first attempt to create a Maskot was in 2007 by the developer Dan Morrill. He tells that he created Inkscape (free software for images vectorization) and created his own Android Maskot version. The purpose was impersonate the system for his team. At the time, there wasn't any request from the company to create a Maskot.

This first bizare version was even baptized in tribute to it's creator, the name was Dandroids.

Comes up a new Maskot

The idea of having a Maskot was getting strenth and the mission was designated to a professional. the illustrator Irina Blok, also Google's employe, was who got the mission to represent the little robot in a more pleasant way.

The main idea was to represent the maskot with few strokes and flattler. The design should also brings a good feeling for who look at it. Comes up the Bugdroid, the new Mascot for the Android system.

The main inspiration for the Bugdroid was one of those toilets doors signs that indicate the gender for each toilet. People says that the artist was in the Google's office when she looked the toilet's door and had the idea for the drawing that was simple, clean and objetive.

Wanna learn more?

Another curious fact about the Android is that every new version of the system has some desert name on it, in alfabetic order from the version 1.5 to 9.0.

  • 1.5 - Cupcake
  • 1.6 - Donut
  • 3.0 - Eclair
  • 2.2 - Froyo
  • 2.3 - Gingerbread
  • 3.0 - Honeycomb
  • 4.0 - Ice Cream Sandwich
  • 4.1 - Jelly Bean
  • 4.4 - KitKat
  • 5.0 - Lolipop
  • 6.0 - Marshmallow
  • 7.0 - Nougat
  • 8.0 - Oreo
  • 9.0 - Pie
